# Install and use the Cisco Proximity App

When Cisco Proximity is turned on, the video conferencing device sends out ultrasound messages for pairing. Nearby devices with the Cisco Proximity client pick up these messages and can easily connect with the video conferencing device without any manual setup.

Cisco recommends deploying the Webex App for wireless sharing, device control and easy meeting join. However, if that's not possible, Cisco Proximity provides a lightweight and device-centric alternative without infrastructure dependencies.

Administrators should read about enabling Cisco Proximity and its services [here ](https://help.webex.com/n1v31g9/Cisco-Proximity-4-0-Cloud-Pairing).

Cisco has discontinued the development of the Cisco Proximity application. This complimentary tool will no longer receive software updates or support. We recommend transitioning to the [Webex App](https://help.webex.com/pceb9t/), [Miracast®](https://help.webex.com/kfdwufb/), or [Apple AirPlay](https://help.webex.com/ndlsg3c/) for secure wireless sharing. For more details, please visit our [support forum](https://community.cisco.com/t5/cisco-proximity/important-update-discontinuation-of-cisco-proximity/m-p/5208465). Thank you for your understanding and continued support. 

Open the app when you're close to a Proximity enabled video system. It will connect to an enabled device and pair automatically. Note that your computer and the video device need to be on the same network. 

- To share your screen on the video system: click Share Screen  or use the keyboard shortcut Alt+F12 . Click Stop Sharing  on the app to end the share.
- To place a call using the app click Make a Call . Search for the person or dial an address. Then click Call . You'll see the regular call controls on your computer screen and can Share , adjust the Volume , Mute , type on the Keypad  and End  the call.

Cisco Proximity doesn't work outside the meeting room when doors to the room are closed. You need to be close to the video device to use the feature.
