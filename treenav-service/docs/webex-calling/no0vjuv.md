# Webex App | Call someone in your organization

- [Desktop](#id%5F125255)
- [iPhone and iPad](#id%5F125260)
- [Android](#id%5F129023)

You can make a call from many different places in Webex App. Just look for the Audio  or Video  call icons whenever you're [sending a direct message](https://help.webex.com/article/zi4dpc), looking at a [contact card](https://help.webex.com/article/n239bv9), or entering a name or email address into the [Search](https://help.webex.com/article/iecbvc) bar. 

If you're set up with a phone service such as Unified CM, Webex Calling, or Calling hosted by a Service Provider, you also have access to a dial pad and can make a call from there. Just click  and enter a person's name, email address, or video address (such as for a room device) in the dial pad.

| 2 | Choose whether you want to make it an Audio ![](https://cisco-api.ingeniuxondemand.com/DITA/content/en/us/td/i/400001-500000/420001-430000/429001-430000/429940.jpg) or Video ![](https://cisco-api.ingeniuxondemand.com/DITA/content/en/us/td/i/400001-500000/430001-440000/438001-439000/438668.jpg) call and then select the person's Work number, Mobile number or Video Address. If you have multiple phone lines (Unified CM only), you can [change the active line for calling](https://help.webex.com/nw31ae5/). After your call connects, you get slightly different options depending on whether you choose someone's work number, mobile number or video address. If you've called their work number or video address as a video call, you can turn the [video on and off](https://help.webex.com/article/nhyyrhz) and, if available, [share your screen](https://help.webex.com/article/xtc8md). But, if you've chosen someone's mobile number, the call goes through like a regular audio-only phone call, which means you can't turn the video on or share your screen. With either work numbers, mobile numbers or video address, you can click ![](https://cisco-api.ingeniuxondemand.com/DITA/content/en/us/td/i/400001-500000/450001-460000/450001-451000/450687.jpg) to get access to advanced calling features. You can put your call on [hold](https://help.webex.com/article/npvltok) or [transfer](https://help.webex.com/article/5er4f3) the person to someone else. With some configurations, you can also [merge](https://help.webex.com/article/v4h0h4) calls or start a [conference call](https://help.webex.com/article/nx5rj7s). If your administrator has enabled call security for your organization, you'll see ![](https://cisco-api.ingeniuxondemand.com/DITA/content/en/us/td/i/400001-500000/440001-450000/442001-443000/442685.jpg) at the top right of your calling window, letting you know that the call is secure. |
