# Webex App | Accessibility features for Mac

## Navigation

Use Tab and Shift + Tab to navigate between areas in the app. You know an area is in focus when it’s surrounded by a blue outline, and you can do the following: 

- Use Up and Down arrows to navigate through messages, spaces, or other menu items in your app.
- Use Space to select items.

## Use a screen reader

You can also use the built-in screen reader on your Mac to navigate conversations, read messages, and access information. To enable, go to System Preferences \> Accessibility \> VoiceOver.

The screen reader informs you when you’re entering certain areas and how to perform actions, such as:

- When you enter the compose message area, the screen reader reads Write a message to… and then provide the name of the space you’re in.
- In the compose message toolbar, the screen reader tells you to use Control + Option + Space/Enter to select and activate an option.
- When you select a message, the screen reader reads the sender's name, time, date, if the message is pinned or flagged, how many thread replies the message has, the number of reactions, and how to reply to the thread.
- In the message reactions menu, the screen reader reads out the different reactions available and offers the option to add a reaction.

## Messaging

When you have focus is in the message area, use Up and Down arrows to move through messages in a space. You can then use the following shortcuts:

- Shift + Up arrow to quickly edit your last message.
- Control + Shift + N to scroll to new messages.
- Control + Shift + R to scroll to new replies.
- Shift + Down arrow to scroll to the bottom of the space.

### Actions on a message

With your focus on a message, use Enter to select the message. You can then use these one-key shortcuts to quickly take actions:

- S to forward the message.
- Delete to remove/delete the message (if it's your message or you're a space moderator).
- Esc to clear selected messages.

### Actions on a card message

With your focus on a card, use Enter to access the card content. Use Esc to go back to the message.

### Compose a message

When you move focus to the [compose message area](https://help.webex.com/article/jc7z88/), use Tab to highlight the first option on the toolbar. 

You can use Right and Left arrows to navigate through the toolbar options, such as: [Attachment](https://help.webex.com/article/yuwad5/), Screen capture, [Format text](https://help.webex.com/article/nbf0ydx/), [Emojis and symbols](https://help.webex.com/article/2eawbp/), [Gifs](https://help.webex.com/article/n07u9hh/), [@ Mentions](https://help.webex.com/article/p5k20o/), and My Personal Room invite. 

To select an option, use Control + Option + Space/Enter.

## ECM files

### Actions on ECM file edit mode

With your focus on an ECM file viewer, use Enter to access the web view content. Use Esc to go back to the main window.

## Keyboard shortcuts

Use Command + / to see a list of some of the available keyboard shortcuts. For detailed information, see [Keyboard Navigation and Shortcuts](https://help.webex.com/article/7wr87q/).

## Make your self-view video accessible to VoiceOver

VoiceOver is a built-in screen reader for macOS that describes what appears on the screen out loud. During your meeting, VoiceOver describes the meeting window and the content of the meeting.

In meetings, this feature is available on our Webex Suite meeting platform. [Find out if your meetings use the Webex Suite meeting platform](https://help.webex.com/m61d8eb/).

Before you begin

On your Mac, turn on VoiceOver and turn on Keyboard navigation in System Settings \>  Keyboard.

| 1 | During the meeting, press F6 until you highlight your self-view video. Your screen reader describes your self-view video.              |
| - | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 2 | To highlight your self-view video while sharing content, press Shift + Command + Control + S until you highlight your self-view video. |
| 3 | Press Tab to select items within your self-view video.                                                                                 |
| 4 | Use the Arrow keys to move your self-view video around the screen.                                                                     |
